I am a **Ph.D. Candidate in Engineering** at Universidad del Bío-Bío, Chile, specializing in **Data Engineering and Geospatial AI**. My work focuses on bridging the gap between advanced machine learning architectures and critical environmental challenges.

## Research Overview
My research focuses on developing geospatial machine learning systems for hydrogeological and environmental modeling in data-scarce regions. 

In my doctoral dissertation, I design spatiotemporal architectures that integrate:
* **Physics-Informed Machine Learning (PINNs)** for robust aquifer forecasting.
* **LSTM-based time-series models** to capture complex hydrological dynamics.
* **Multi-source data fusion**, combining satellite remote sensing (MODIS, Landsat, Sentinel), seismoelectric geophysical signals, and sparse piezometric observations.

This research addresses critical water security challenges in climate-vulnerable agricultural zones of Latin America, aligning with global efforts in **Machine Intelligence** and **Climate Sustainability**.

## Professional Experience & Innovation
Beyond academia, I am deeply involved in translating deep-tech research into deployable, real-world solutions:

* **Geospatial AI Systems Architect at G-STRATA SpA**: Leading the design of AI-driven systems for groundwater intelligence, including the SEMq-1 geophysical sensor and the G-WaterLink platform.
* **Founder & Technical Lead at IDESAT**: Driving R&D for educational aerospace systems and robotics. Our project was selected for the *Brain Chile* National Deep-Tech Innovation Program in 2024.

Previously, I have contributed to CubeSat attitude control analysis at the University of Chile and developed aerospace recovery systems in Silicon Valley and Boston.
